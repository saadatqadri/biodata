Getting started with GitHub Pages and Pelican

Date Fri 03 December 2010 Tags pelican / publishing

I started writing a blog earlier this year, with the intention of capturing my ideas and fleeting thoughts. I initially started a Tumblr blog, wasn't too happy with the themes available for free, and then switched over to Svbtle, a new, minimalist writing platform by Dustin Curtis. I was really excited about the minimalist feel of Svbtle at first, and some of the people I'm following (Nima Gardideh (Frank & Oak)) maintained a Svbtle blog. However, the lack of certain features - adding Disqus comments, and the ability to modify the base Svbtle theme was a bit limiting. I wanted people to comment on my articles. I wanted to elicit discussion.

Long story short - I moved my content from Svbtle to Medium, and I started looking at options for hosting a "landing page" for myself. I played around with Ghost and Wordpress, but I just wanted a simple way to get my content online. After a bit of research, GitHub Pages was what I was looking for.
